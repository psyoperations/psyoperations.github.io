Phising

and PsyOps

difference

Phishing and Psyops (psychological operations) are two different concepts, but they do share some similarities.

While phishing is a type of cyber attack that involves tricking individuals into divulging sensitive information or downloading malware, psyops is a type of military or intelligence operation that aims to influence people's behavior or beliefs through propaganda, disinformation, and other psychological tactics.

Psyops awareness training

is a type of training program designed to educate individuals and organizations about the risks and consequences of psychological operations (psyops). Psyops are a type of operation where psychological tactics, including propaganda, disinformation, and other techniques, are used to influence attitudes and behaviors in a target population.

Identify

How to identify psyops attempts: This may include teaching individuals to look out for suspicious or unusual messages or information that are designed to manipulate or misinform, including propaganda, fake news, and deepfakes.

Media

Best practices for media literacy: This may include teaching individuals to be critical consumers of media, to fact-check information before sharing or acting on it, and to be aware of their own biases and assumptions.

Behavior

Tips for safe online behavior: This may include teaching individuals to be aware of their digital footprint, to protect their privacy and personal information, and to use secure communication channels when necessary.

Psyops awareness training typically involves teaching individuals how to identify psyops attempts and other types of influence operations, as well as how to respond to these threats.

Psyops awareness training may also include simulated psyops attempts, where individuals are exposed to mock psyops messages or other types of influence operations to see how they respond. These simulated attacks can help to reinforce the training and identify areas where individuals may need additional education or support.

PsyOps simulation

A psyops simulator - a tool designed to test and train individuals or organizations' ability to detect and respond to psychological operations. It might include simulations of propaganda, misinformation, or other tactics used in psyops campaigns, and would be designed to help individuals or organizations recognize and resist such operations.

Like phishing simulators, psyops simulators could be used by military or intelligence organizations to train their personnel to recognize and resist psychological operations. They could also be used by other organizations, such as governments, corporations, or non-profits, to assess their susceptibility to such operations and take steps to mitigate the risk.
